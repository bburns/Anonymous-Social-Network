ASN3

Specification

ASN is an attempt at an anonymous social network that tracks a student's academic interests and displays the academic interests of others.

There are three types of users:

  1. administrators
  2. students
  3. browsers

Administrators can do the following:

  • run unit tests
  • import/export the data
  • remove a user and its data
  • reset the system

Students can do the following:

  • create an account (ID and password randomly generated by the system)
  • log in with randomly generated ID and password
  • change password
  • add/edit/remove classes (unique, course number, course name, semester, instructor, rating, grade)
  • add/edit/remove books (isbn, title, author, rating)
  • add/edit/remove papers (journal or conference, title, author, rating)
  • add/edit/remove internships (company, location, semester, rating)
  • add/edit/remove places to study (location, semester, rating)
  • add/edit/remove places to live (location, semester, rating)
  • add/edit/remove places to eat (location, semester, rating)
  • add/edit/remove places to have fun (location, semester, rating)
  • add/edit/remove games (os, title, rating)

Browsers can see lists of the following and for each, the number of ratings and the average rating:

  • courses (average grade)
  • books
  • papers
  • internships
  • places to study
  • places to live
  • places to eat
  • places to have fun
  • games

In this phase of the project your group needs to do the following:

  • Polish your application. In particular, have it as good as you can by Wed, 17 Nov, 10pm. At that time other groups will be exercising and rating the GAE incarnation of your app, and that will contribute to your grade.
  • Collaborate with the other groups in the class and arrive at a single XML instance for the whole class and import that into the GAE. This will be in the form of 11 posts in Blackboard, one from each group.
  • Revise the technical report to include the following:
    • Title
      • Names of each of the team members
    • Introduction
      • What is the problem?
      • Why is it interesting and important?
      • Why is it hard? (E.g., why do naive approaches fail?)
      • What are the key components of our approach and results? Also include any specific limitations?
      • Which team member is most responsible for each of the major components of the project?
    • Related Work
      • How does our solution compare to others?
      • Review each of the other 10 group GAE apps and identify the following:
        • three things they did better
        • three things you did better
        • three bugs
        • a rating (1-10) of the other group's app
    • Design
      • What were our design decisions?
      • What special features did we add?
      • Roles: admins, students, browsers
      • UI
      • UML
      • XML
    • Implementation
      • What is our solution?
      • Frameworks and tools
      • Authentication and session management
      • Datastore
      • Forms
      • Validation
      • Import/Export
      • Files and directory structure
    • Evaluation
      • How did we test our solution?
      • unit tests
      • performance tests
      • groupware tests
    • Conclusion
      • Why is our solution the best?
      • outstanding issues
      • future improvements
      • What did we learn?
  • Give an in-class presentation/demonstration with the following:
    • title, group members
    • browsing, filtering, searching
    • logging in as an admin, and performing admin functions
    • logging in as a student, and viewing, editing, and adding data
    • issue tracker, major bugs fixed, outstanding issues, future improvements
    • a persuasive argument about why your group's design and implementation is the best
    • what did you learn

Groups

Pick a member of the group to be project leader. A group member can only be project leader once.

Once the TA has graded the project, that will be the project grade.

The project leader will always receive the project grade.

The project leader can add or subtract up to 10 points to or from any member of the group in exchange for adding or subtracting the same amount of points from the other member(s) of the group.

For example, the leader could add 10 points to person A, and subtract 5 points from person B and person C.

The leader will report these adjustments in the Google Form.
